# Site Type Patterns

Quick-reference patterns by site type, compiled from researching 30+ real sites across 4 categories. Use this as a starting point — then validate with actual site visits for the specific niche and geography.

---

## SaaS Product Sites

**Based on:** 8 SEO SaaS sites (Ahrefs, Semrush, Moz, Surfer, Mangools, Serpstat, SE Ranking, Nightwatch)

### Table Stakes Pages
- Homepage (value prop + demo/animation + social proof + CTA)
- Pricing (3-4 tiers, monthly/annual toggle, "Most Popular" badge, enterprise "Contact Sales")
- Features / Product pages (one page per major feature, not one big features list)
- Blog (active, SEO-driven)
- Help Center / Documentation
- Login / Signup
- Privacy Policy, Terms of Service
- About / Company

### Differentiators
- **Free tools** (7/8 have them) — standalone landing pages targeting "[tool] free" queries. The #1 growth lever for SaaS. Each free tool is a signup funnel.
- **Academy / Courses** (6/8) — free certifications beat blog posts for differentiation and brand advocacy
- **API Documentation** (6/8) — developer-facing, separate from main docs
- **Integrations directory** (5/8) — each integration gets its own page

### Content Strategy
- Blog: YES (all 8 have active blogs)
- Academy/courses: STRONG YES (6/8, high differentiation)
- Free tools: STRONG YES (7/8, #1 growth lever)
- Podcast: occasional (2/8)
- YouTube: common (5/8)

### Pricing Pattern
- 3-4 tiers: Starter ~$50, Professional ~$100-200, Enterprise ~$400-500+
- Monthly/annual toggle (17-27% annual discount)
- "Most Popular" badge on middle tier
- Free trial: 5/8 no credit card required
- Enterprise: "Contact Sales"

### Legal
- Privacy Policy (8/8)
- Terms of Service (8/8)
- GDPR/DPA page (5/8)
- Cookie consent (6/8, EU-focused)
- Security page (4/8)

### Gaps / Opportunities
- ROI calculator (0/8)
- PPP/localized pricing (1/8)
- Public status page (2/8)
- Accessibility statement (0/8)
- AI editorial/data methodology transparency (0/8)

## Local Business Sites

**Based on:** 10 sites across dental, plumbing/HVAC, law firms, salons in US cities

### Table Stakes Pages
- Homepage (hero + services preview + trust signals + CTA)
- Services hub page
- Individual service pages (8-24 pages — the #1 SEO pattern)
- About Us / Team (with credentials)
- Contact (phone + form + map)
- Reviews / Testimonials
- Privacy Policy

### Industry-Specific Pages
| Industry | Unique pages |
|---|---|
| Dental | Before/after gallery, insurance accepted, emergency info |
| Legal | Practice areas (deep, 3 levels), case results/verdicts, free consultation |
| Plumbing/HVAC | 24/7 availability, service areas, coupons/specials, financing |
| Salon/Spa | Online booking, before/after gallery, team bios with specialties |

### Differentiators
- **City/service area landing pages** (only 2/10 — biggest SEO opportunity)
- **FAQ page with 15+ questions** (top rankers have them, most don't)
- **Video content** (4/10, correlation with higher engagement)
- **Schema markup** beyond basic Organization (FAQ, Service, Review schema)

### Contact Patterns
- Phone in header: 100% of sites
- Contact form: 100%
- Online booking: 80% (dental + salon especially)
- Dual CTA for emergency services: "Call Now" + "Schedule Online"
- Live chat: 30% (higher for emergency services)
- WhatsApp: 0% in US, dominant in Latin America

### Content Strategy
- Blog: mixed (6/10 have one, only 3 active)
- FAQ: YES — top rankers have 15-24 questions
- Video: recommended for trust-building
- Case studies/results: YES for legal, dental before/after

### Legal
- Privacy Policy (8/10)
- Terms of Service (4/10)
- Accessibility statement (0/10 — legal risk + easy win)
- Professional license numbers: displayed by all regulated businesses
- Insurance/payment info: common for medical

### Gaps / Opportunities
1. City landing pages (strongest local SEO tactic, rarely done)
2. Accessibility statements (legal risk, nobody has one)
3. Deep FAQ pages (15+ questions capture long-tail traffic)
4. Schema markup (FAQ, Service, Review schema rarely implemented)
5. Video content (builds trust, rarely used well)

## Content / Media Sites

**Based on:** 7 sites (The Verge, Epicurious, Serious Eats, Healthline, Investopedia, Backlinko, Wirecutter-style)

### Table Stakes Pages
- Homepage (latest + featured + category navigation)
- Article template (author, date, TOC, related articles, newsletter CTA)
- Category / Topic archive pages
- About page
- Author bio pages (with credentials, article archive, social links)
- Search
- Privacy Policy, Terms of Service
- Editorial process / standards page
- Contact

### Differentiators
- **Expert review badges** (Healthline: "Medically reviewed by Dr. X") — huge trust signal for YMYL content
- **Content hubs / pillar pages** (Backlinko, Investopedia) — topic clusters with hub-and-spoke internal linking
- **Interactive tools / calculators** (Investopedia, NerdWallet) — drive traffic and engagement
- **Structured data** (FAQ, HowTo, Article, Review schema)

### Content Organization
- **Primary axis: Topic/category** (not format or recency)
- Hub pages that link to all related articles
- Breadcrumbs showing topic hierarchy
- "Related articles" at end of every post
- Tags for secondary classification

### Monetization Visible
- Display ads (5/7)
- Affiliate links (3/7 — Wirecutter, NerdWallet, Backlinko)
- Sponsored content (2/7)
- Newsletter (7/7 — universal)
- Paywall (1/7 — rare for content sites, common for news)

### Trust / Credibility
- Named authors with photos on EVERY article (7/7)
- Editorial process page explaining how content is created (5/7)
- Expert reviewer credits for health/finance (YMYL) content
- Corrections/updates policy
- Source citations within articles
- "Last updated" dates (more important than publish dates)

### Newsletter Strategy
- Signup in 3-5 locations per page (header, mid-article, end-of-article, footer, dedicated page)
- Value prop emphasizes brevity: "5-minute read", "daily digest", "the week's best"
- Universal across all content sites — the #1 conversion action

### Legal
- Privacy Policy (7/7)
- Terms of Service (7/7)
- Editorial Policy / Standards (5/7)
- Advertising Disclosure (4/7)
- Accessibility statement (2/7)
- Cookie consent (5/7, EU-aware sites)

### Gaps / Opportunities
- AI content disclosure policy (1/7 — emerging requirement)
- Community features (0/7 — discussion moved to social)
- Personalized content recommendations (2/7)
- Audio versions of articles (1/7)

## B2B News / Industry Publications

**Based on:** 8 sites (Search Engine Land, SEJ, TechCrunch, The Information, Morning Brew, AdExchanger, Digiday, Sifted)

### Table Stakes Pages
- Homepage (latest news, featured stories, topic sections)
- Article template (author, date, share buttons, related, newsletter CTA)
- Topic category archives
- About page
- Author / contributor bio pages
- Newsletter signup (dedicated page + inline everywhere)
- Contact
- Advertising / Sponsorship page (media kit)
- Events / Webinars section
- Search
- Privacy Policy, Terms of Service

### Differentiators
- **Proprietary data / research reports** (1/8 — massive opportunity)
- **Community** (Slack/Discord — 0/8 have owned community)
- **AI editorial policy** (1/8 — emerging requirement)
- **Corrections policy page** (0/8 — trust opportunity)

### Content Organization
- **Topic-first navigation** (8/8) — organized by industry vertical, not by content type
- Latest/breaking at the top of homepage
- Featured/editor's picks highlighted
- Content types mixed within topics (news + analysis + opinion in the same feed)

### Revenue / Monetization
- Display advertising (8/8)
- Sponsored content / native advertising (6/8)
- Events / conferences (6/8)
- Premium / paywall tier (3/8)
- Job board (2/8)
- Research reports (1/8)

### Newsletter
- THE #1 conversion action (8/8)
- 3-5 signup placements per page
- Value prop: brevity + daily delivery
- Multiple newsletters by topic (3/8)
- Popup/modal on first visit (4/8)

### Author Attribution
- Named author with photo on EVERY article (8/8)
- Author bio pages with social links + article archive (7/8)
- Non-negotiable for editorial credibility

### Comments
- Dead (0/8 have active comments)
- Discussion has moved entirely to LinkedIn and X/Twitter
- Share buttons instead of comment sections

### Legal
- Privacy Policy (8/8)
- Terms of Service (8/8)
- Advertising Disclosure (5/8)
- Editorial independence statement (3/8)
- Cookie consent (6/8)
- AI editorial policy (1/8 — emerging)
- Corrections policy (0/8 — gap)

### Gaps / Opportunities
1. Corrections policy page (0/8 — trust signal)
2. AI editorial policy (1/8 — becoming expected)
3. Owned community (0/8 — Slack/Discord)
4. Proprietary data products (1/8 — revenue opportunity)
5. Audio/podcast versions of articles (2/8)

## Cross-Category Patterns

### Universal (appears in ALL 4 site types)
- Privacy Policy
- About page
- Contact mechanism
- Newsletter / email capture (for content-driven sites)
- Mobile-responsive design
- Named attribution (authors for content, team for local business, founders for SaaS)

### Legal pages that should ALWAYS exist
- Privacy Policy — every site type, every country
- Terms of Service — every site with user accounts or form submissions
- Cookie consent — if targeting EU or using tracking cookies

### The "comments are dead" pattern
- 0/8 B2B news sites have active comments
- 0/7 content sites use comments as a primary engagement mechanism
- Discussion has moved to social platforms
- Don't build a comments section unless the community demand is proven

### Content strategy varies DRAMATICALLY by site type
| Site type | Blog? | FAQ? | Academy? | Free tools? | Newsletter? |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| SaaS | Yes (SEO) | Sometimes | Yes (differentiator) | Yes (#1 growth) | Yes |
| Local business | Maybe | Yes (SEO) | No | No | No |
| Content site | IS the product | Sometimes | No | Yes (calculators) | Yes (#1 conversion) |
| B2B news | IS the product | No | No | No | Yes (#1 conversion) |
