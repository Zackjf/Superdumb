# Discover Competitors

Before you can study what works, you need to find the sites to study. This skill produces a research list of 15-25 real websites to visit and analyze.

## Three Categories of Sites to Find

### 1. Direct Competitors (10-15 sites)
Same business type, same or similar geography.

**Search queries to run:**
- `[business type] en [city]` (in the local language)
- `[business type] [city]` (in English, if the market is bilingual)
- `mejor [business type] [city]` / `best [business type] [city]`
- `[business type] near [neighborhood/zone]` (if the city is large)

**What to record for each result:**
- URL
- Business name
- Google ranking position for the query
- Google Reviews rating + count (if visible in SERP)
- Brief first impression (professional, outdated, modern, sparse)

**Filter:**
- Skip directory listings (Yelp, Yellow Pages, Google Maps results pages)
- Skip social-media-only businesses (Instagram page but no website)
- Keep only actual business websites
- Include a mix: top-ranking (positions 1-5), mid-ranking (6-15), and any that look notably good or bad

### 2. Best-in-Class (3-5 sites)
The best websites globally for this business type, regardless of geography. These show what "great" looks like.

**Search queries:**
- `best [business type] website design`
- `best [business type] website examples`
- `award-winning [business type] website`
- `[business type] website inspiration`

**What to record:**
- URL
- Country/market
- Why it appeared (award, ranking, design showcase)
- First impression

### 3. Adjacent Local Businesses (5-10 sites)
Other service businesses in the same city/market. These reveal LOCAL web patterns — do businesses in this market use WhatsApp? Do they show prices? What's the design quality baseline?

**Search queries:**
- `[other service type] en [city]` — try 3-4 different service types
  - For a dental clinic: search for lawyers, dermatologists, veterinarians, salons
  - For a restaurant: search for hotels, event venues, catering, bars
  - For a SaaS: search for other SaaS tools in the same category
- `mejor [other service] [city]`

**What to record:**
- URL
- Business type
- Contact channel used (WhatsApp, phone, form, email, booking system)
- Whether prices are shown
- Overall design quality (1-5 scale)
