# Opportunities — What Nobody Else Is Doing

Competitor research tells you what to match. This skill tells you what to BUILD BEYOND the market.

After visiting 15-25 sites, you know what everyone does. Now ask: **what's missing? What should exist but doesn't? What would make a visitor choose THIS site over all the others?**

## How to Find Opportunities

### 1. Gap analysis — what's missing from ALL competitors?

Go through the common page types and content types. If NONE of the competitors do something that would clearly help their customers, that's a gap.

Examples:
- No competitor has a price calculator → build one
- No competitor answers "what to expect at your first visit" → create that page
- No competitor has a page in English for expats → build one
- No competitor shows real-time availability → add it
- No competitor has video consultations → offer it
- None have accessibility features → be the first

### 2. Bad pattern analysis — what do ALL competitors get wrong?

Look for things every competitor does badly:
- All have outdated designs → modern design is a differentiator
- All have slow sites → fast site stands out
- All require phone calls to book → online booking is an advantage
- All hide prices → showing prices builds trust
- All have stock photos → real photos feel authentic
- All have generic "About" pages → a real story stands out
- None have emergency contact clearly visible → add an emergency banner

### 3. Adjacent market innovations

What do the BEST-IN-CLASS sites (from other markets) do that local competitors don't?

- US dental sites have virtual consultations → could this work locally?
- UK sites have patient review aggregation → could this build trust locally?
- Best-in-class sites have interactive treatment guides → could a simplified version work?

### 4. Underserved audiences

Who visits this type of business but isn't served by any competitor's website?

- Expats who don't speak the local language → bilingual pages
- Elderly patients who are less tech-savvy → simplified contact (big phone number, not WhatsApp)
- Anxious patients → "What to expect" pages, virtual tour
- Price-sensitive patients → payment plans page, insurance info
- Parents looking for pediatric services → dedicated pediatric section

### 5. Content gaps

What questions do people ask (Google PAA, Reddit, forums) that NO competitor's site answers?

Search for:
- `[business type] reddit [common question]`
- `[business type] [city] forum`
- Google "People Also Ask" for main keywords
- Review sites (Google Reviews, Yelp) — what do people mention in reviews that the website doesn't address?

Turn unanswered questions into pages or FAQ entries.

### 6. Feature ideas

Think about what the website could DO beyond being a brochure:

| Category | Possible features |
|---|---|
| **Booking** | Online scheduling, WhatsApp appointment bot, callback request |
| **Pricing** | Price calculator, treatment comparison, payment plan estimator |
| **Education** | Treatment guides, "what to expect" pages, video walkthroughs |
| **Trust** | Real-time Google Reviews feed, before/after gallery, video testimonials |
| **Convenience** | Multi-language support, pre-visit forms (fill paperwork online), virtual consultation |
| **Local** | Parking/transit info, neighborhood guide, nearby pharmacy list |
| **Engagement** | Dental health quiz, smile assessment upload, referral program |
| **Retention** | Appointment reminder setup, patient resources portal, post-treatment care guides |

**Filter each idea through two questions:**
1. **Would this actually help the target user?** A dental health quiz sounds fun but does a person with a toothache want to take a quiz? Probably not. A "what to expect" page? Yes.
2. **Is this worth the build cost?** A full booking system is complex. A WhatsApp pre-filled message achieves 80% of the value at 5% of the cost. Pick the simple version.

## Output Format

```
OPPORTUNITIES:

GAPS (things no competitor does that would help):
1. No competitor has a "What to expect at your first visit" page
   → Build this. Reduces anxiety for new patients. Good for SEO
   ("que esperar en mi primera cita con el dentista").

2. No competitor shows prices upfront
   → 0 of 15 show prices. But price is the #1 concern (seen in
   Google reviews). Showing "Desde $25" builds trust and pre-qualifies.

3. No competitor has a page for dental emergencies
   → 8 of 15 mention emergencies but none have a dedicated page.
   "Emergencia dental" has search volume. Build a simple page:
   symptoms to watch for, what to do, emergency phone number prominent.

IMPROVEMENTS (things competitors do badly):
4. All 15 sites use stock photography
   → Use real clinic + team photos. Immediate trust advantage.

5. Average page speed is 5.2 seconds
   → Build a fast, static site. Under 2 seconds = standout.

6. Only 2 of 15 are mobile-friendly
   → Mobile-first design = immediate advantage for 90% phone users.

FEATURE IDEAS (worth building):
7. WhatsApp appointment request with pre-filled message per service
   → Low effort, high value. Each service page has its own WhatsApp
   link: "Hola, quiero agendar una cita para blanqueamiento"

8. Before/after gallery with consent-approved photos
   → 5 of 15 have this, all rank in top 8. High trust signal for dental.

9. FAQ page based on actual patient questions
   → Captures long-tail search traffic. 15 questions from Google PAA
   and competitor reviews.

DEFERRED (interesting but not for v1):
10. Online booking calendar — complex, WhatsApp achieves 80% of the value
11. Virtual consultation — good idea but needs infrastructure
12. Patient portal — 0 of 15 have this, not expected in the market
```

## Rules

- **Every opportunity must explain WHY it's worth doing.** Not just "add a blog" — "add a blog because [evidence]" or "skip the blog because [evidence]."
- **Rank by impact/effort.** Quick wins first (pre-filled WhatsApp links), complex builds last (booking system).
- **Include a "Deferred" category.** Not everything needs to be in v1. Good ideas that aren't worth the build cost yet go here.
- **Be honest about what's a nice-to-have vs. what moves the needle.** A dental health quiz is a nice-to-have. A "what to expect" page actually reduces no-shows. Focus on the latter.
